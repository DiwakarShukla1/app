AT IMTTL WE BELIEVE THAT SOIL ANALYSIS SHOULD BE REALISTIC, HOLISTIC AND DETERMINISTIC!

Soil analysis is a tool that has been employed with the primary goal of providing recommendations for soil rectification, crop productivity and/or soil health management. Conventional methods Diethylene Triamine Penta Acetic Acid and ammonium acetate extraction are commonly used for analysis of bioavailable nutrients. However, there are limitations to these methods as both extraction fluids are buffered to neutral or near-neutral pH.Hence extracted nutrients represent a “potential or ideal-case” fertility status of soil instead of an “actual” field status.

IMTTL SOIL AUDIT: pH as the Yardstick for Analysis:

pH is called the master variable in the soil which means it is THE controlling factor as far as nutrient solubility and plant availability of nutrients in the soil is concerned. Ionic activity (supply of nutrients to roots) as well as the “cation and anion ionic capacity” (the release of nutrient ions from a fixed form to available form) are both pH dependent.

Hence, the validity and agronomic dependence on the pH chart. With the exception of phosphorus, which has a small available pH range, macronutrients (N, K, Ca, Mg, and S) are more available within a pH range of 6.5 to 8, while the majority of micronutrients (B, Cu, Fe, Mn, Ni, and Zn) are more available within a pH range of 5 to 7. This trend should reflect in all soil audits and hence at IMTTL we have adopted a modified method in which the extraction fluid is buffered to actual soil pH to get a REALISTIC soil-audit of the available nutrients in the soil.

  • For ACIDIC soils we use the MEHLICH 3 reagent for extraction of nutrients and finally adjust the extract to pH of the soil in question.
  • For ALKALINE soils we use the DTPA/Ammonium Acetate reagents for extraction of nutrients and finally adjust extract to pH of the soil in question

IMTTL SOIL AUDIT

The following parameters are included in IMTTL Comprehensive Soil Audit:

  1. Soil Rectification Parameters:pH, Ec, Carbonates, Texture, Na, Cl, Ca, Mg, K, OM%, WHC and FC.
  2. Crop Productivity Soil Analysis:N,P,K, Ca ,Mg, S, Fe, Mn, Zn, Cu, B, CEC, % Base Saturation. Aluminium is included as a parameter for acidic soils.
  3. Microbiological Enumeration.
    1. Probiotic Profile: (Beneficial Bacteria):
      1. Total Fungal Count
      2. Total Bacterial Count
      3. Total Actinomycetes count
      4. Diazotrops (Free Nitrogen Fixing Bacteria)
      5. Phosphorus Solubilising Microbes
    2. Proteobacteria: (Harmful Enteric Bacteria): E.Coli, Salmonella and Pseudomonas
  4. Biochemical Parameters:
    1. Urease Activity: Urease is the enzyme that catalyses the hydrolysis of urea to ammonium and carbon-dioxide. This enzyme is widely distributed in nature and its activity is highly correlated to activity of microbes in the soil. Urease helps in converting organic nitrogen into plant available nitrogen.
    2. Phosphomonoesterase Activity: Phosphomonoesterases represents a broad group of enzymes that catalyze hydrolysis of both esters and anhydrides of phosphoric acid. The phosphomonoesterases- acid and alkaline phosphatase are important in the mineralization of soil organic phosphorus and making inorganic phosphorus available to the plant.
    3. Fluorescein Diacetate Activity: Fluorescein diacetate (FDA) is a substrate that is hydrolysed by lipases, proteases and esterases- enzymes which are involved in the microbial decomposition of organic matter in soil. It is a direct indicator of microbial acivity in the soil and is used as an indicative parameter for carbon cycling in the soil.
  5. Detailed and Customized Recommendations based on crop and soil in question.
